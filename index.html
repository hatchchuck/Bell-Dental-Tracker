Bell Dentistry · Delta Dental · HSA

Dental Claim Tracker

Bell Dentistry is paid in full out of pocket at time of service — not from the HSA. Bell is non-participating with Delta Dental, so it isn't bound to Delta's fee schedule and can charge more than what shows as the "accepted fee" on the EOB. The net cost is based on what you actually paid Bell, not Delta's benchmark — and that net amount is the one pulled from the HSA once the claim settles.

How this works
  1. Pay out of pocket. Bell Dentistry is paid its actual charge at time of service, from checking or a card — not the HSA.
  2. Claim processes. Delta Dental calculates its payment off its own "accepted fee" benchmark — not Bell's actual bill, since Bell never agreed to that fee schedule. Delta reimburses part of it directly to you.
  3. Net cost is now known. What you actually paid Bell minus what Delta paid you = the real out-of-pocket cost. This can run higher than the EOB's "you pay" line, since that line only reflects Delta's benchmark, not any balance Bell billed above it.
  4. Pull that net amount from the HSA, once. Since the HSA is only reimbursing genuinely uncompensated cost, there's nothing to repay and nothing to report on Form 8889.

You can reimburse yourself from an HSA for a qualified expense at any point after it's paid — there's no deadline — so there's no rush once a visit shows "Ready to withdraw." Confirm specifics with your HSA administrator or tax preparer.
